Lights That ATTRACT Attention (in a Good Way)
Using bright and contrasting colors can capture attention and stimulate visual interest in people with dementia. Simple visual activities like painting or looking at colorful things can also provide a creative outlet and promote relaxation. PubMed Central
Visual stimulation for people with dementia can involve light, color, shape, or motion — or a combination of all of them. Gently animated lights, kaleidoscopes, colorful paintings, nature movies, and fiber optic lights are all good examples. Visual stimulation is essentially brain stimulation.
